Cashback earned at Tier 1 but not yet claimed by the participant. Computed from CRM rows where Status = "Redeemable", with the reward dollar value from the T&C Section 4 reward table, and cross-verified against Col M "Redeemed Counpons" = 0 for these rows.
In plain terms: this is the dollar amount of cashback that participants have already earned but haven’t claimed yet. TMGM is contractually committed to paying $310 to specific clients, but those clients haven’t clicked redeem on their coupons yet. So the money sits in a “promised but not yet paid out” state.
The 31 participants here have met the Tier 1 threshold per T&C Section 4 (deposited $100 or more and traded at least 1 lot), so they qualify for the $10 cashback. The reason it’s “owed” not “paid” is they haven’t hit redeem yet — the $10 hasn’t moved to their trading account.
Where this sits in the cashback trio: Cashback Paid ($11,530) + Cashback Owed ($310) = Total Liability ($11,840). The Total Liability is TMGM’s full April cashback exposure as of the May 5 snapshot.
What happens next: per T&C Section 4(h) Example 1, participants have until 30 September 2026 at 23:59:59 to redeem. If they redeem in time, the $10 moves from Owed to Paid. If they don’t redeem by the deadline, the cashback is forfeited and TMGM no longer owes it. The $310 figure will change in the May, June, and post-September monthly reports as redemptions occur or the deadline passes.

Campaign at a Glance

The 2026 Q2 Asia Client Cashback Promotion runs from 1 April 2026 to 30 June 2026 inclusive (T&C Section 1, deposit period). This is a three-month deposit window during which eligible clients can register and earn cashback by progressing through three sequential tiers.

This report uses the CRM export dated 5 May 2026, capturing all activity in April 2026 — the first of three calendar months in the deposit period. Activity in May and June 2026 is out of scope for this April monthly report.

The reward structure (T&C Section 4) is $10 cashback at Tier 1, $150 at Tier 2, and $500 at Tier 3, each gated by progressively higher cumulative deposit and trading-lot thresholds. A participant must claim each tier in sequence; T1 must be claimed before working toward T2.

What the Data Shows

April activity was heavily concentrated in five days. The window from 13 to 17 April (Mon–Fri) contained 1,176 of 1,376 applications (85.47%), with a single-day peak of 350 applications on Wednesday 15 April. The other 25 calendar days in April combined produced 200 applications. The cause of this concentration is not determinable from the source columns alone. See Section 5 for the full daily breakdown.

The tier funnel is steeply tapered. 1,124 of 1,376 (81.69%) earned Tier 1, and 1,093 of those 1,124 (97.24%) have already claimed it — high engagement at the entry tier. From Tier 1 onward the conversion drops sharply: only 4 of 1,093 (0.37%) have gone on to claim Tier 2, and 0 have claimed Tier 3. The remaining 1,089 Tier-1 claimers are in Status “Stage 2” — working toward Tier 2 deposit and lots thresholds. See Section 3.

Activity is concentrated by midweek and by Web platform. Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday combined account for 1,057 applications (76.82%); Saturday and Sunday combined for 42 (3.05%). 1,260 of 1,376 applications (91.57%) originated from the Web platform; 116 (8.43%) from the APP platform. See Section 2 and Section 5.

Data integrity is strong on critical fields. All 1,376 April rows have non-blank values in Cols A (Member Name), B (Email), C (Trading Account), D (Application Platform), E (Application Time), and F (Status). Email is unique per row (1,376 distinct emails). Member Name has 35 duplicate-name groups covering 99 rows (7.19%) — one cluster requires compliance review (see Items Flagged for Attention below). See Section 4.

Items Flagged for Attention

Five items surface in this report. These are observations only; this report does not propose action items.

The “Ribun Bunyamin” name cluster contains 29 of 1,376 April rows (2.11%), all with distinct emails — uniform 1:1 same-name-to-different-email pattern. This is the largest of 35 duplicate-name clusters in April. Flagged for compliance review under T&C Section 8 (promotion manipulation, market abuse or misconduct, or unfair advantage); deferred, not adjudicated. Recommended next step is KYC verification before settlement. See Section 4 Block B and Section 6 Finding 7.

A single row holds 12 trading account IDs (the maximum observed across all 1,376 April rows). 140 rows (10.17%) hold more than one trading account ID. Whether this represents legitimate multi-product trading or a pattern requiring review is not determinable from the source columns alone. See Section 4 Block C and Section 6 Finding 8.

The Apr 13–17 application spike (85.47% of monthly volume in 5 days) has no causal explanation derivable from the source columns. Whether this reflects a marketing push, a regional campaign trigger, a registration deadline, or another driver is for the operations or marketing team to confirm. See Section 5.

$310 of cashback is owed but not yet claimed — 31 rows are in Status “Redeemable”, meaning they have qualified for Tier 1 ($10 each) but have not yet redeemed the coupon. Per T&C Section 4(h), the redemption deadline for Q2 Asia coupons is 30 September 2026 at 23:59:59. See Section 2 Card 3.

The CRM source file contains no country attribution column. Per-country breakdowns of applications, cashback, or tier progression cannot be derived from the source files alone.
